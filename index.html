About Me

Committed to health systems that leave no one behind.

My work sits at the intersection of implementation science, delivery science, and health equity advocacy - translating evidence into systems that protect the most structurally marginalized. I have co-designed Rwanda's iCLM framework for HIV, TB, and malaria; driven national SRH policy reform; and co-founded Medconnekt Pharmacy, a stigma-free pharmaceutical delivery model for populations that conventional health systems routinely fail.

As Founder and Executive Director of Health Bridge Rwanda, I am building an NGO dedicated to bridging structural gaps in health equity - spanning six programs pillars and 18 flagship projects anchored in community voice, digital innovation, and rights-based care.

My analytical lens in the Reverse Assumption Framework - surfacing the structural logic hiding beneath standard public health assumptions to unlock solutions that conventional thinking cannot reach.

AI in Lagos State Transportation System: Barriers, Current State and Future Potentials - published

Lagos State is the most populous state in Nigeria and Africa as a continent. Due to its large population, commuters struggle with traffic congestion, long waiting and commuting hours, and a high rate of accidents. This paper explores how AI can be used to effectively manage the state's transport systems.

Machine Learning Framework for Characterizing Marine Microbial Enzymes with Microplastic Biodegration Potential - in peer review

The goal of this study was to develop and evaluate a machine learning framework that can characterise the function of marine microbial enzymes with microplastic biodegradation potential - based on optimal temperature, pH, and Enzyme Commission number classification.

A Distinguisher-Resistant Honey Encryption Scheme Using BERT Model for Information Security - manuscript in preparation

Modern encryption schemes rely on the intractability of certain mathematical problems. However, with the rapid growth in computational power, problems that are difficult today may become solvable in the future. To address this risk, we propose a honey encryption scheme.
